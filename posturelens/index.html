PostureLens

External security posture report cards — DNS, email authentication, TLS, and subdomain takeover signals for any public domain. Runs entirely in your browser.

No domains tracked yet
Tracks up to 15 domains. Each scan takes ~15–25 seconds and runs client-side using public DNS-over-HTTPS, Certificate Transparency logs, and the Chromium HSTS preload list.

How the score works

Every domain is graded A–F from a composite 0–100 score, weighted across four areas. Every finding below is observable from the public internet — no credentials, no authenticated probes.

Email authentication

35%

Spoofed email is the most common vector for credential phishing and BEC fraud. Strong SPF + DMARC alignment is the cheapest, highest-impact control a domain owner has.

  • DMARC policy at _dmarc.<domain>: reject +25, quarantine +15, none +5, missing −30.
  • SPF qualifier: -all +15, ~all +8, +all (open) −25, missing −15.
  • SPF DNS-lookup count > 10 (RFC 7208 limit): −10.
  • DMARC rua= reporting tag missing: warning, no point penalty.
  • MTA-STS record at _mta-sts.<domain>: +10 if present.

Sub-score starts at 50 and is clamped 0–100. DKIM is not checked — selectors aren't discoverable externally.

Attack surface

35%

Unintended public exposure of internal tooling and dangling subdomain takeovers are routinely exploited. We enumerate via Certificate Transparency logs (CertSpotter).

  • Sensitive-pattern subdomains with publicly issued certs (vpn, admin, jenkins, jira, grafana, kibana, vault, etc.): −4 each, capped at −24.
  • Subdomain takeover candidates — CNAME points to a known third-party service whose target doesn't resolve: −12 each, capped at −40.
  • Total subdomain count: reported, not scored.

Sub-score starts at 70. Takeover detection samples up to 50 subdomains and is a candidate signal — manual verification required.

DNS hygiene

20%

DNSSEC prevents resolver-level spoofing; CAA limits which CAs can mint certs; nameserver redundancy is basic resilience.

  • DNSSEC (AD flag set on apex A query): +25.
  • CAA record at apex: +15.
  • Nameservers ≥ 2: +10. Only one: −10.

Sub-score starts at 50. AD flag relies on Google Public DNS validation.

Web security

10%

Intentionally a light weight — browser CORS prevents us from reading TLS or HTTP security headers directly, so we can only verify reachability and preload membership. For full TLS analysis, the detail panel links to Qualys SSL Labs.

  • HTTPS reachable (TLS handshake completes on apex or www): +30. Unreachable on both: −25.
  • HSTS preload list membership (Chromium upstream): +15.

Sub-score starts at 50.

Grade thresholds

A85 – 100 — Strong external posture across all four areas.
B70 – 84 — Solid baseline, a couple of hardening opportunities.
C55 – 69 — Material gaps, prioritize fixes.
D40 – 54 — Multiple exploitable misconfigurations likely.
FBelow 40 — Critical failures (e.g., no DMARC, open SPF, active takeover candidate).
