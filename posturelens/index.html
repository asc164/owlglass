How PostureLens scoring works

The grade

Each domain receives a composite score from 0–100 and a letter grade (A: 85+, B: 70–84, C: 55–69, D: 40–54, F: under 40). The score starts at 50 (industry-typical baseline) and adjusts up or down based on four sub-scores.

Sub-scores

Email authentication (weight: 35%). SPF presence and strictness, DMARC presence and policy enforcement, MTA-STS deployment. Weighted heavily because email spoofing is a direct, frequent attack vector and these signals are reliably observable from public DNS.

Attack surface (weight: 35%). Subdomain count and presence of sensitive subdomain patterns from public Certificate Transparency logs. Includes subdomain takeover detection: CNAME records pointing at deprovisioned third-party services (GitHub Pages, Heroku, S3, Azure, Shopify, and others) are checked against a fingerprint database. Detected takeover candidates carry a steep penalty because they represent immediate, exploitable risk.

DNS hygiene (weight: 20%). DNSSEC validation, CAA records, nameserver redundancy. Indicates operational maturity but rarely causes breaches on its own.

Web security (weight: 10%). HTTPS reachability and HSTS preload list membership. Weighted lightly because deep TLS analysis requires capabilities a browser cannot provide; the "SSL Labs deep scan" link runs a more thorough audit on demand.

Honest limits

Takeover detection is capped at the 50 most-recently-issued subdomains per domain to keep scans fast. The fingerprint database covers ~20 services where takeover is a documented risk. We do not see internal patching, EDR, or any controls behind authentication. Web security relies on browser-accessible signals only; advanced TLS analysis requires the SSL Labs deep scan.
