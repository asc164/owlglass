How PostureLens scoring works

The grade

Each domain receives a composite score from 0–100 and a letter grade (A: 85+, B: 70–84, C: 55–69, D: 40–54, F: under 40). The score starts at 50 (industry-typical baseline) and adjusts up or down based on five sub-scores.

Sub-scores

Email authentication (weight: 25%). SPF presence and strictness, DMARC presence and policy enforcement, MTA-STS deployment.

DNS hygiene (weight: 15%). DNSSEC validation, CAA records, nameserver redundancy.

Web security (weight: 15%). HTTPS reachability, HTTPS enforcement (HTTP redirects to HTTPS), and HSTS preload list membership. The "SSL Labs deep scan" link runs a more thorough TLS audit on demand.

Attack surface (weight: 15%). Subdomain count and presence of sensitive subdomain patterns (admin, dev, jenkins, vpn, etc.) found in public Certificate Transparency logs.

Vendor exposure (weight: 30%). Cross-references technologies detectable from your DNS records (mail provider via MX, hosting hints via NS) against the live CISA Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog. Recent KEV matches lower this sub-score sharply.

Honest limits

Vendor matching is heuristic substring matching against KEV vendor names — produces some false positives. We do not see internal patching, EDR, or controls behind authentication. Web security relies on browser-accessible signals only; advanced TLS analysis requires the SSL Labs deep scan. Industry-tagged threat intelligence is not used because most public threat data is not reliably industry-tagged.
