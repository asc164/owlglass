What SurfaceLens checks

Read this if you want to understand what each finding means and where the data comes from.

Subdomain discovery

We query the public CertSpotter Certificate Transparency API for every TLS certificate ever issued to the domain. Each certificate's SAN entries become candidate subdomains. Coverage is excellent for HTTPS-facing assets but misses hosts that have never had a public cert.

DNS resolution

Each discovered subdomain is resolved through Google DNS-over-HTTPS for current A and AAAA records. Unresolvable hosts are kept in the inventory but marked as inactive — they may have been decommissioned.

Open ports & CVEs

Every unique IP is looked up in Shodan InternetDB, a free passive scan dataset refreshed weekly. We surface open ports, known CVEs and service tags. Data is passive — we never scan you or your assets directly.

Organization discovery

Mode B groups SANs from your seed's certificates by registrable domain, then scores candidates by how often they co-occur with the seed, the certificate issuer quality, exclusivity, and name similarity. Multi-tenant issuers (Let's Encrypt on shared platforms) are downweighted to reduce noise.

Sensitive hostnames

Hostnames containing tokens like admin, staging, dev, internal, vpn, jenkins, git are flagged. These are often non-production assets that leaked into Certificate Transparency and warrant a closer look.

Limitations

Browser-only means no port scanning, no banner grabbing, no screenshots. Shodan InternetDB data may be days to weeks old. Subdomains without a cert are invisible to CT-based discovery. Mode B is heuristic — always verify candidates before treating them as authoritative.
